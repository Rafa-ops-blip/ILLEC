About us

Indigenous language learning centre (ILLEC) is one of the institutions designed to improve the indigenous languages in our educational sector and outside it. Its establishment can be seen as part of the commitment to building a solid foundation for the realization of the educational and cultural policies of Niger State. It is in an attempt to produce a lasting solution to the problems listed below that the establishment of ILLEC in 2019 happened:

To encourage the learning of Nigerian languages in an environment which shall prepare the students to speak the language fluently and acquire proficiency in teaching the Nigerian languages

To acquire competence in research for solution to problems on teaching Nigerian languages in all levels of education in Nigeria

To award certicficates (diploma and others) to qualified students

Collaborators

The collaborators of the Nupe language and literacy organization are a diverse group of linguists, scholars, and community members, united by a shared commitment to the preservation and advancement of Nupe culture. Together, they contribute their expertise in linguistics, literature, and community engagement, forming a dynamic and dedicated team working collaboratively to achieve the organization's goals. HOW ILLEC STARTED

ILLEC started from Manarakis' effort to start teaching Nupe Language unconventionally on his own without a teacher to guide him. Manarakis relied on the available Nupe books to guide him. The books were written by British Anthropologists, Nupe Historians, Nupe Researchers and so on. He also consulted old Nupe adults in different communities of Nupe land.

On 19th November, 2010, Manarakis was inspired by an article he read written by a British Anthropologist; A.W. Banfield. He took the idea to Facebook with a little poem. Interestingly, that Nupe poem fueled his energy to kick-start a research on Nupe Language. The poem was loved by many friends on Facebook which attracted about 400 likes and comments.

MANARAKIS FACTOR IN ILLEC

Adamu Idris Manarakis is the founder of ILLEC. He was never a literature student from the beginning. He studied Building Technology from Eyagi Technical College, Bida, then Building Technology Education from College of Education, Minna, then the same Building Technology Education at his first Degree programme from Federal University of Technology, Minna. After a Post Graduate Diploma programme in Nigerian Languages, he is currently studying Masters Degree in Linguistics with Nasarawa State University (NSUK), Keffi.
